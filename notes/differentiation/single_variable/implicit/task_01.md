$x \cdot y = -8$, $\frac{dy}{dt} = -6$. Find $\frac{dx}{dt}$ when $x=-4$

First let's take a look at:

$$(x \cdot y) = -8$$

Substituting $x = -4$:

$$y = \frac{-8}{-4} = 2$$

Now let's go back to the main problem:

$$\frac{d}{dt}(x \cdot y) = \frac{d}{dt}(-8)$$

$$\frac{dx}{dt} y + x \frac{dy}{dt} = 0$$

$$\frac{dx}{dt} \cdot 2 + -4 \cdot -6 = 0$$

$$\frac{dx}{dt} = -12$$
